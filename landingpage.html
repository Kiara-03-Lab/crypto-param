FAQ

Common questions

LWE (Learning With Errors) is a mathematical problem that's believed to be hard even for quantum computers. It's the foundation of most post-quantum encryption schemes, including Kyber (now a NIST standard). If you're building secure systems that need to survive the quantum era, you're probably using LWE.
Our estimates are based on the same methodology used by academic cryptographers (specifically, the Lattice Estimator). They're accurate within ~10% for the primal attack, which is typically the most effective attack. For production use, we recommend consulting with a cryptographer.
This tool estimates plain LWE security. Kyber and Dilithium use Module-LWE, which has additional algebraic structure. Their security is typically lower than plain LWE with the same parameters. For structured lattice schemes, use the full Lattice Estimator.
128 bits of security means an attacker would need to perform 2^128 (about 340 undecillion) operations to break your encryption. For reference, if you tried a billion operations per second, it would take longer than the age of the universe. It's the industry standard for "secure enough."
